# Awesome neural/psychological manifolds

A curated list containing neural manifold articles on top neuroscience-related journals. The list is in an alphabetical order in APA citation style. 0 means the most important and the earliest discovery.

0. **The FAMOUS Mel Scale of Pitch in Psychoacoustics describing the neural manifold (functional, or representation-learning based, geometric objects emerged from neural population firing) of human cochlear**: [Stevens, S. S., Volkmann, J., & Newman, E. B. (1937). A scale for the measurement of the psychological magnitude pitch. The journal of the acoustical society of america, 8(3), 185-190.](https://pubs.aip.org/asa/jasa/article-abstract/8/3/185/673917/A-Scale-for-the-Measurement-of-the-Psychological) 
1. [Adams, R. A., Napier, G., Roiser, J. P., Mathys, C., & Gilleen, J. (2018). Attractor-like dynamics in belief updating in schizophrenia. Journal of neuroscience, 38(44), 9471-9485.](https://www.jneurosci.org/content/38/44/9471.short) <i>HGF, hierarchical guassian filter, Friston, active inference, attractor</i>
2. [Boccato, T., Testolin, A., & Zorzi, M. (2021). Learning numerosity representations with transformers: Number generation tasks and out-of-distribution generalization. Entropy, 23(7), 857.](https://www.mdpi.com/1099-4300/23/7/857) <i>A pixel-level Transformer learns a conditionally disentangled representation of numerosity and generates dot images containing both trained and previously unseen numerosities. Its main contribution is demonstrating that an approximate cardinal-number geometry can support interpolation and extrapolation beyond the training distribution. For clinical psychology, the takeaway is that continuous clinical constructs could be explicitly conditioned, disentangled from nuisance factors, and evaluated through out-of-distribution generalization rather than only in-sample prediction.</i>

Biological Psychiatry: Cognitive Neuroscience and Neuroimaging, 10(5), 463-474.](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2451902224001733) <i>Novel, using manifold to model the interaction between dimensions and contexts. [EPHATE git repo](https://github.com/ericabusch/EPHATE)</i>

11. [Ferrante, M., Boccato, T., Ozcelik, F., VanRullen, R., & Toschi, N. (2024). Through their eyes: Multi-subject brain decoding with simple alignment techniques. Imaging Neuroscience, 2, 1–21.](https://direct.mit.edu/imag/article/doi/10.1162/imag_a_00170/120739/Through-their-eyes-Multi-subject-brain-decoding) <i>This study aligns one participant’s fMRI activity with another participant’s functional space and then applies a decoder trained on the reference participant. Its main contribution is showing that simple ridge-regression alignment using approximately 10% shared stimuli can approach within-subject decoding performance while greatly reducing subject-specific data requirements. For clinical psychology, the takeaway is that patient-specific alignment layers may support a shared group-level neural manifold without assuming that all patients have identical neural representations.</i>
